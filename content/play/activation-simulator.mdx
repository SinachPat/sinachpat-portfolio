## The problem

When I'm working through an onboarding problem, I usually end up with a messy mix of Figma frames, sticky notes, and spreadsheets. None of them let me simulate the user's experience as a flow — with friction scores, drop-off points, and the moment activation actually happens.

I wanted a single canvas to think this through.

## What it does

The Activation Simulator lets you map an onboarding flow as a sequence of steps. For each step you can:

- **Label it** — what the user sees or does
- **Score the friction** — how hard is this step? (1–5 scale)
- **Mark the aha moment** — the point where the user first gets value
- **Flag drop-off risk** — steps likely to lose users

The canvas gives you an instant read on where your onboarding is overloaded and how far users have to go before they reach the payoff.

## Current state

This is still in progress. The core step-mapping flow works. What's left:

- Drag-to-reorder steps
- Export to a shareable PNG summary
- Add branching paths (not all onboarding flows are linear)

## Why I'm building it

A lot of activation work happens in spreadsheets because there's no lightweight visual tool for it. I'm not trying to replace Miro or FigJam — I'm trying to build something you can open in 10 seconds and hand to a PM in a meeting.

Fast, opinionated, focused on one job.
