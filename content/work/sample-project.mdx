## Overview

Acme Corp was losing over 60% of signups in the first week. Users weren't reaching the "aha moment" — the point where they understood the product's core value. My goal was to redesign the entire onboarding experience to get users to that moment faster.

## The Problem

When I joined the onboarding project, the team had 14 onboarding screens — most of which were collecting information Acme didn't actually need yet. Users dropped off at step 3 consistently.

The hypothesis was simple: we were asking for commitment before we'd demonstrated value.

## Research

I ran 12 user interviews across three user segments. Key findings:

- Users didn't understand the product's core value from the marketing site
- The signup form asked for company size and budget before users had tried anything
- The "getting started" flow buried the most valuable feature 6 clicks deep

## The Solution

I proposed a "value-first" onboarding model:

1. **Reduce friction to first action** — 3 steps to create an account, no credit card required
2. **Guided first session** — interactive walkthrough that shows value in under 5 minutes
3. **Progressive data collection** — ask for company info only after the user has completed their first meaningful action

## Outcome

The redesigned flow shipped in Q2 2024. Within 30 days:

- Activation rate jumped from 22% to 61%
- Time-to-value dropped from 14 days to 3
- Support tickets related to onboarding dropped by 67%

The success came from a simple insight: people need to understand value before they're willing to invest effort.
